Big Lots Reports June, 2003 Sales.
Big Lots Inc. reported retail sales for the five weeks ended July 5, 2003 of $373.6 million, a 9.9% increase compared to retail sales of $340.1 million for the same period of 2002. Comparable store sales for all stores that have been open at least two years as of the beginning of the fiscal year increased 5.0% in June, with the number of customer transactions increasing 2.6% and the value of the average basket increasing 2.4%. The June comparable store sales increase of 5.0% was on top of last year's industry leading 15.7% increase. Retail sales increased 7.7% to $661.4 million for the nine-week quarter to date period ended July 5, 2003, compared to $614.2 million for the same period in fiscal 2002. Comparable store sales increased 2.9% for the quarter to date period with the number of customer transactions increasing 1.9% and the value of the average basket increasing 1.0%.
