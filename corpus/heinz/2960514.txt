HJ Heinz Co. Workers Plan to Strike.
HJ Heinz Co. workers plan to strike at the company's British headquarters to protest plans to close the HP Sauce factory in Birmingham, England, and shift production to the Netherlands. The group, from the Transport and General Workers Union, will form a protest line with banners and signs as labor leaders meet company managers. It announce 125 jobs are at risk. Company officials plan to meet with a half-dozen employees and union representatives after Heinz announced May 5 it may close the factory in March 2007. The possible closing is part of Heinz's review of manufacturing operations after last year's takeover. According to the union, Heinz's plans to move jobs to the Netherlands represents a breach of the commitments they gave to us when they bought the company last year.
