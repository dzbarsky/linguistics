Kimberly-Clark Corp. Reports Earnings Results for the Second Quarter of 2008.
Kimberly-Clark Corp. reported earnings results for the second quarter of 2008. The company reported second quarter profit fell 10%, confirming pre-released results that showed it had been hurt by rising gas and distribution costs. The company has earned $416.7 million, or 99 cents per share, down from $461.8 million, or $1 per share a year ago. Revenue is up 11.2%, to $5 billion from $4.5 billion. Excluding one-time costs, it earned $1.03 per share. The results suffered due to a sharp rise in costs for natural gas, oil-based materials and distribution.
