The New York Times Company Reports Unaudited Consolidated Earnings Results for the Fourth Quarter and Full Year Ended December 31, 2010; Provides Revenue Guidance for the First Quarter of Fiscal 2011; Provides Capital Expenditure Guidance for the Full Year 2011.
The New York Times Company reported consolidated earnings results for the fourth quarter and full year ended December 31, 2010. For the quarter, the companys total revenues decreased 2.9% to $661.7 million from $681.2 million in 2009. Operating profit excluding depreciation, amortization, severance and the special items discussed declined 7.1% to $146.4 million compared with $157.6 million in the fourth quarter of 2009. On a GAAP basis, the company had an operating profit of $111.6 million compared with $136.0 million in the fourth quarter of 2009. The company reported net income attributable to company of $67.1 million or $0.44 per diluted share compared to net income attributable to company of $90.9 million or $0.61 per diluted share a year ago. Income from continuing operations attributable to company was $67.1 million or $0.44 per diluted share compared to income from continuing operations attributable to company of $71.5 million or $0.48 per diluted share a year ago.
