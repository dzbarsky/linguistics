The New York Times Company Reports Consolidated Earnings Results for the First Quarter Ended March, 2011; Provides Earnings Guidance for 2011.
"The New York Times Company reported consolidated earnings results for the first quarter ended March, 2011. For the quarter, the company reported operating profit of $31,074,000, income before income taxes of $6,632,000, net income of $5,226,000, net income attributable to the company of $5,419,000 or $0.04 diluted per share on total revenue of $566,504,000 against operating profit of $52,658,000, income before income taxes of $41,185,000, net income of $14,158,000, net income attributable to the company of $12,793,000 or $0.08 diluted per share on total revenue of $587,867,000 for the same period a year ago. Capital expenditures totaled approximately $10 million in the first quarter of 2011. Excluding severance and other non-recurring items, the company earned 2 cents per share. The company reported total advertising revenues in March further softened partly in response to the uneven economic climate. The company reported earnings guidance for 2011.
