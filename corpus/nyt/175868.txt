The New York Times Co. Reports Earnings Results for the Fourth Quarter and Full Year of 2001.
"The New York Times Co., said that its fourth-quarter net income fell 46 percent as a weak advertising market more than offset cost cuts and gains in circulation income. The Times Company, which owns publications including The Times and The Boston Globe, posted net income of $74.1 million, or 48 cents a share, down from $137.8 million, or 83 cents a share, in the quarter a year earlier, when the sale of three regional newspapers bolstered income. Revenue fell to $780.6 million from $927.1 million. Profit in the quarter came to $76.9 million, down 31.5 percent from $112.1 million a year earlier. Calculated per share, the figure fell to 50 cents from 68 cents. For all of 2001, the company reported a profit of $1.59 a share, excluding special items, down from $2.10 in 2000. The company's broadcast group said revenue fell 19.3 percent, to $38.1 million, in the quarter, while operating profit fell 38.4 percent, to $10.8 million. In 2000, the company's television stations had benefited from political advertising"
