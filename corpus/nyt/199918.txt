NY Times Appoints Washington Bureau Chief.
New York Times Co, has named Philip Taubman its Washington, D.C., bureau chief. Taubman, deputy editor of the newspaper's editorial page, will replace Jill Abramson, who was recently appointed a managing editor, the newspaper announced. Taubman, 55, was named to his current position in 2002. He also has served as assistant editorial page editor, deputy national editor, Washington editor and Moscow bureau chief.
