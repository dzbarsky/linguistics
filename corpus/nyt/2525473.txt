New York Times Co. Announces Executive Changes.
New York Times Co. announced that Charles Strum has been named associate managing editor for the night side and Paul Winfield has been named news editor. Strum's appointment will take effect once his replacement has been announced and Winfield's appointment is with immediate effect. Strum is currently obituaries editor, a post he has held for the past five years. Prior to his current role Strum oversaw the Public Lives column and held several roles on the Metro desk, including New Jersey bureau chief. Winfield has been deputy news editor since 1994. He has worked at The Times in Sports for 19 years beginning as a clerk and then worked on the Metro desk before moving to the News desk.
