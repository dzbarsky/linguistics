New York Times Co. Appoints Michael Rogers to Serve as Consultant to Research and Development Unit for One Year.
New York Times Co. named Michael Rogers as futurist-in-residence, a newly created position where he will serve as a consultant to the research and development unit for one year. Rogers, is an interactive media executive, novelist and journalist, who founded the Practical Futurist, a consulting firm that works with startups and media companies. He writes a column for MSNBC with the same name. Rogers has also served as vice president of the Washington Post Co.'s new media division from 1995 to 2004 and as editor and general manager of Newsweek.com.
