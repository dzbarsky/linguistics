Starbucks Receives Approval to Open Store at the Maryland Inn.
Starbucks Corp. announced a decision by the Annapolis Historical Preservation Commission to approve architectural plans for the store at the Maryland Inn means 21st century visitors will be able to chat over coffee at a site where Washington, according to local lore, once lost a horse in a game of cards. The commission approved the plans after it received assurances the site's history will be respected and that it will be wheelchair accessible. The building, originally the King of France Tavern, was a haunt of American patriots and revolutionaries. Parts of it still have the original brick floor, stone walls and cooking fireplace.
