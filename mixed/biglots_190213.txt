Big Lots Reports July Comparable Store Sales And For Fiscal Year 2004 Second Quarter.
"Big Lots Inc. reported retail sales for the four weeks ended August 2, 2003 of $277.3 million, a 9.3% increase compared to retail sales of $253.8 million for the same period of 2002. Comparable store sales for all stores that have been open at least two years as of the beginning of the fiscal year increased 4.5% in July, with the number of customer transactions increasing 2.9% and the value of the average basket increasing 1.6%. The July comparable store sales increase of 4.5% was on top of last year's 14.4% increase. Retail sales increased 8.1% to $938.7 million for the fiscal year 2004, quarter ended August 2, 2003, compared to $868.0 million for the same period in fiscal 2002. Comparable store sales increased 3.4% for the quarter with the number of customer transactions increasing 2.3% and the value of the average basket increasing 1.1%. The planned elimination of one advertising circular during May reduced comparable store sales for the quarter by an estimated 1%. For the twenty-six week year to date"
