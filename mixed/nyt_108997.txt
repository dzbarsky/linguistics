The New York Times Co. announced third quarter ended 2003 results.
"The New York Times Co. reported a 15 percent decline in third quarter earnings. It earned $50.1 million in the three months ending in September, compared with $59 million in the same period a year ago. On a per-share basis, the earnings were equivalent to 33 cents, a penny better than Wall Street analysts had been expecting, according to Thomson First Call. The company earned 38 cents per share in the same three-month period last year. Total revenues rose 4 percent to $759.3 million versus $729.5 million. For the first nine months of the year, net income edged lower to $191.8 million from $192.2 million in the same period a year ago. Revenues rose 5 percent to $2.34 billion from $2.24 billion."
