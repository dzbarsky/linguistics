The New York Times Company Reports Consolidated Earnings Results for the Second Quarter and Six Months Ended June 27, 2010; Provides Capital Expenditure Guidance for the Year 2010.
"The New York Times Company reported consolidated earnings results for the second quarter and six months ended June 27, 2010. For the quarter, the company's total revenues were $589.6 million compared with $582.7 million a year ago. Operating profit was $60.8 million compared with $23.5 million a year ago. Income from continuing operations before income taxes was $57.0 million compared with $1.0 million a year ago. Income from continuing operations was $31.6 million compared with $39.2 million a year ago. Net income attributable to the company common stockholders was $32.0 million or $0.21 per diluted share compared with $39.1 million or 0.27 per diluted share a year ago. Income from continuing operations attributable to the company stockholders was $31.8 million or $0.21 per diluted share compared with $39.2 million or $0.27 per diluted share a year ago.
