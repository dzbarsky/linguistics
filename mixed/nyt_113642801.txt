The New York Times Company Provides Earnings Guidance for the Third Quarter of 2010 and Capital Expenditures Guidance for the Year 2010.
"The New York Times Company provided earnings guidance for the third quarter of 2010. The company anticipated results for the third quarter of 2010 compared to the same period in 2009. Total revenues to decrease approximately 2% to 3%. The company expects to report a third-quarter diluted loss per share from continuing operations in the range of $0.05 to $0.07 per share. Excluding severance costs and the two charges diluted earnings per share are expected to be approximately $0.03 to $0.05 per share. The third-quarter results will include the special items of a $16 million ($10 million after tax or $0.07 per share) charge for a write-down of assets at The Boston Globe's printing facility in Billerica, Mass. and a $6 million ($4 million after tax or $0.03 per share) charge for an adjustment to estimated pension withdrawal obligations under several multi-employer pension plans at The Boston Globe.
