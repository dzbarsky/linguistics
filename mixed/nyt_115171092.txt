The New York Times Company Announces Offering of Senior Notes Due 2016.
The New York Times Company announced that it intends to offer $200 million aggregate principal amount of senior notes due 2016. The company intends to use the net proceeds for general corporate purposes including, among other things, to pay down debt and other financial obligations as part of the company's refinancing strategy. The notes are to be offered and sold to "qualified institutional buyers" as defined in Rule 144A under the Securities Act of 1933, as amended and outside the United States in reliance on Regulation S under the Securities Act.
